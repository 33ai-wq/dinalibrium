## 1. Abstract

Dinalibrium is an open-source web app for analyzing any tradable asset using the principle of **Dynamic Equilibrium** — borrowed from physics and chemistry, applied to financial markets.

The core idea: every market is a battleground of opposing forces. When those forces balance, the asset is in equilibrium. When they do not, price moves predictably toward balance.

Dinalibrium quantifies this with a single number: the **DES Score**.

---

## 2. The Problem

Most trading tools rely on pattern-matching indicators like RSI or MACD. They tell you what happened — not where the asset stands relative to its natural balance point.

Dinalibrium answers a different question: *"Is this asset in equilibrium right now — or is it out of balance?"*

---

## 3. Dynamic Equilibrium in Markets

In chemistry, dynamic equilibrium is when two opposing reactions occur at equal rates. In markets:

- Buy Pressure vs Sell Pressure
- Momentum vs Mean Reversion
- High Volatility vs Stability

When these forces are balanced, the asset is at its Dinalibrium point. When one side dominates, price will correct back toward balance.

## 4. The DES Score

| DES | Zone | Meaning |
|-----|------|---------|
| 0 – 20 | Extreme Oversold | Selling force exhausted |
| 20 – 42 | Under-Equilibrium | Below balance point |
| 42 – 58 | Dinalibrium | Perfectly balanced |
| 58 – 80 | Over-Equilibrium | Above balance point |
| 80 – 100 | Extreme Overbought | Buying force exhausted |

---

## 5. The Four Forces

**Momentum Ratio (30%)** — strength of buying vs selling direction over 24 hours.

**Volume Pressure (25%)** — whether volume is weighted toward buyers or sellers.

**Volatility Inverse (25%)** — lower volatility means closer to equilibrium.

**Mean Reversion Pull (20%)** — how far price has drifted from its historical average.

---

## 6. Signal Output

- Signal: Strong Buy / Buy / Neutral / Sell / Strong Sell
- Strength: 1 to 5 bars
- Confidence: percentage across all four forces
- Entry, Stop Loss, Take Profit — from real-time volatility
- Risk:Reward — minimum 1:2.5
